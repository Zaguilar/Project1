Traveling During a Pandemic

We all know that the COVID 19 Pandemic impacted us all from every angle of our lives. As humans progressed throughout the decades we became more and more intertwined and less separated by distance. Meaning businesses, families, events have become essential to a global economy. As the world slowly opens up again to travel it is important to make informed travel decisions. With our webpage you will be able to search for flights and consider COVID 19 API risks and current status in a foreign country.

    General Safety Tips 06/02/2020

    Clean your hands often. Wash your hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after you have been in a public place, after touching surfaces frequently touched by others, after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing, and before touching your face or eating. If soap and water are not available, bring and use hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol. Cover all surfaces of your hands and rub your hands together until they feel dry. Avoid touching your eyes, nose, or mouth with unwashed hands. Avoid close contact with others. Keep 6 feet of physical distance from others. Wear a cloth face covering in public. Cover coughs and sneezes. Pick up food at drive-throughs, curbside restaurant service, or stores. Make sure you are up to date with your routine vaccinations, including measles-mumps-rubella (MMR) vaccine and the seasonal flu vaccine. - CDC (https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/travelers/faqs.html)



    Traveling Restrictions 06/02/2020

    If you are traveling abroad to a foreign country you should pay attention to their current travel restrictions. Some countries require all inbound travelers to self-quarantine for 14 days. Other countries may have more strict visa requirements. You should always consult and stay contstantly up to date on the specific requirements and changes made by each respective country.
